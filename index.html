Questions

Fair questions, straight answers.

Is my gold real?+
Yes. Every unit is backed 1:1 by physical, investment-grade gold in professional Swiss vaults. Reserves are independently attested and published, and your holdings are verifiable on-chain anytime.
Does Auri hold my money?+
No. Your gold and cash sit in a wallet only you control, secured by passkeys. Auri is software — if anything ever happened to Auri, your assets would still be yours.
I'm not into crypto — is this complicated?+
You pay in Canadian dollars and see dollars, grams and ounces. No tokens, no addresses, no gas fees. The machinery stays under the hood — checkable if you're curious, invisible if you're not.
What's the catch with borrowing?+
Liquidation risk. If gold's value falls far enough against your loan, part of your gold can be sold to repay it. Borrow conservatively — your loan health is shown live, always.
Is Auri a bank? Is my money insured?+
No — Auri is a non-custodial software platform, not a bank. Balances aren't deposits and aren't CDIC-insured, and gold and crypto assets carry risk. What you get instead is custody: your funds never sit on our balance sheet at all.
What does it cost?+
The exact price, rate and any fee appear on the confirm screen before every action. No hidden spreads, no storage fees, no surprises.
Can I gift gold to someone who doesn't use Auri?+
Yes — send by contact, link, or QR code. They can claim their gold without an Auri account to start.
What about taxes?+
We don't give tax advice, but you can export your full transaction history anytime and hand it to your accountant. Everything is recorded in CAD.

Auri is a non-custodial software platform. Auri is not a bank and is not a deposit-taking institution, and it does not provide banking, brokerage, investment, legal, or tax advice. Auri does not hold custody of your funds; your gold and cash balances are held in a wallet that you control.

Digital assets — including tokenized gold and stablecoins — are not deposits and are not insured by the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) or any government deposit-insurance scheme. The value of gold and crypto assets can go down as well as up, and you can lose money.

Borrowing against gold carries liquidation risk: if the value of your collateral falls, some or all of your gold may be sold to repay your loan. Boost uses leverage, which amplifies both gains and losses and increases the risk of liquidation. Only use leverage you can afford to lose.
