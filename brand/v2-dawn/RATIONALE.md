# Auri — Logo Round 2: Gold that moves

All four concepts are built on one custom letterform system: monolinear geometric
lowercase/caps (Geist-adjacent — near-circular bowls, flat terminals, 26-unit stems on a
100-unit x-height), drawn from scratch so the wordmark is ownable, not a typeset word.
Every concept is flat single-color first (tested in ink `#15161B`); gradient is dress-up only.

## The concepts

**Concept 1 — "Comet A" (wordmark).** Title-case `Auri` in which the A has no ordinary
crossbar: the bar escapes the letter and tapers off to the left as a light-streak, its edges
slightly concave so it thins the way light does, not the way a blade does. The word reads as
having just launched forward; the streak *is* the crossbar, so nothing is decoration. Strong
at every size, but its energy is sharp and aeronautical — more velocity than warmth — and it
re-centres the brand on the letter A, which round 1 already burned.

**Concept 2 — "Dawn tittle" (wordmark) — WINNER.** Lowercase `auri`, and the dot of the *i*
is the whole idea: a gold sun/coin gliding right along a thin light-line, its tapered trail
sweeping back over the word. Lowercase keeps all the airspace above the x-height empty, so
the sky above *auri* belongs to that one moving dot — the site's sun-line motif landing
inside the logotype itself. It is the brand argument in one gesture: the gold is not shut in
a vault under the word; it is up, out, and moving. Reads at 24 px, degrades gracefully
(trail → whisker, dot stays), and distills into a one-glyph mark.

**Concept 3 — "Carried sun" (mark + word lockup).** A semicircular sun grounded on a long
tapered horizon-line, placed right-of-centre so the line reads as travel already covered;
beside it, the plain `Auri` wordmark with a conventional A. The motion is quieter —
rising + gliding rather than streaking. Honest and calm, but the mark lives *next to* the
word instead of inside it, and separate marks earn their keep more slowly than integrated ones.

**Concept 4 — "First light" (wildcard, display-only).** Caps `AURI` sliced clean through by
the horizon light-line — the line passes behind the letters (through the gap) and replaces
the A's crossbar on its way. It is the website hero translated literally: dawn cutting
across a dark word. Cinematic at large sizes; below ~32 px the slice closes up and reads as
strikethrough, so it could only ever be a display variant, never the primary mark.

## Why concept 2 wins

- **It says the strategy.** Round 1 failed because a static ingot says *stored*. Here the
  gold (the dot) is airborne and travelling — spend/send/gift is implied by the physics.
- **Aurum and aurora in one shape.** The dot is both coin and low sun; the trail is both
  motion-blur and dawn light. Auri's double etymology, drawn.
- **It rhymes with the product.** The site's signature gold light-streak now begins in the
  logo — nav, hero, and favicon all speak one line.
- **Everyday, not monumental.** Lowercase, round, warm — money for daily life, backed by
  the seriousness of the geometry.
- **It scales.** One glyph (the dawn-dot *i*) becomes mark, app icon, and favicon with no
  redrawing; at 16 px it is still legibly an *i* with a bright head.

## Usage rules

**Minimum sizes.** Wordmark: 24 px tall (nav) recommended, 18 px absolute floor; below that
use `mark.svg`. Mark/favicon: 16 px floor. Concept 4 (if ever used as a display variant):
32 px cap-height minimum. Print: wordmark ≥ 8 mm tall.

**Clearspace.** Keep a margin equal to the dawn-dot's diameter (the lockup's "coin",
≈ 21% of lockup height) clear on all sides. Nothing may enter the trail's sky — no text or
icons above the word inside the clearspace.

**Color.** One color at a time: ink on light grounds, cream on dark grounds, gold gradient
reserved for hero/celebration moments on the plum-tinted night backgrounds. Never mix two
colors inside the lockup (the dot is always the same color as the word — the motion must
read as one material). Plum `#A12C66` is a product accent, not a logo color; the only plum
in identity assets is the deep-plum favicon tile. Do not add glows or shadows to the vector —
on the website, glow comes from the environment (the hero sun-line), not the file.
